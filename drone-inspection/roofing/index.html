What We Detect

What Our AI Finds on Your Roof

Thermal and visual drone cameras capture what the human eye misses — even from the ground.

🧱

Missing & Damaged Shingles

High-resolution aerial photography identifies cracked, missing, or lifted shingles across the entire roof surface.

🌡️

Thermal Anomalies & Hidden Leaks

Infrared thermal imaging detects moisture intrusion, wet insulation, and active leaks invisible to standard cameras.

💧

Ponding Water & Drainage Issues

AI identifies areas of standing water, blocked drains, and negative slope drainage problems.

🔩

Flashing & Seam Failures

Detects failed flashing around chimneys, vents, skylights, and roof-to-wall junctions — common leak entry points.

🏚️

Structural Sagging & Deformation

3D analysis identifies roof deck sagging, rafter damage, and structural deformation from storm or age.

🌿

Moss, Algae & Debris

Documents biological growth and debris accumulation that accelerates roof deterioration and voids warranties.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How does a drone roof inspection work?
A FAA-certified pilot flies a drone over your roof capturing high-resolution photos and thermal imagery. AI analyzes the footage, detects damage, and generates a detailed report with annotated photos — delivered in 48 hours.
Is drone inspection better than a manual one?
Safer (no ladders), faster (under an hour on-site), and more comprehensive — the aerial perspective captures the entire roof surface systematically, including areas that are inaccessible or dangerous for manual inspection.
What's in the inspection report?
High-resolution aerial photos, thermal imagery, AI-annotated damage locations, defect classifications, a summary narrative, and a repair priority list — all in a branded PDF ready for your insurer or contractor.
How much does it cost?
Residential inspections start at $149. Commercial and multi-family properties are priced by square footage. Contact us for a custom quote.
