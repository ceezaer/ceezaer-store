Why It Matters

Why Austin Real Estate Agents Use Drone Photography

Austin's market has more than 10,000 active listings at any given time. Buyers scroll fast. Aerial shots stop the scroll — they show lot size, neighborhood context, proximity to amenities, and the scope of the property in a way no ground photo can.

📐

Show the Full Property

Ground photos can't show a half-acre lot, a pool + outdoor kitchen setup, or a property's position on a greenbelt. Aerial stills communicate scale and site context instantly — which is exactly what Austin buyers with large budgets want to see before scheduling a showing.

📍

Neighborhood Context

Austin buyers pay a premium for proximity — to the Domain, to downtown, to major employers like Apple, Tesla, and Dell. A well-framed aerial shot showing nearby landmarks communicates location value better than any text description.

🏆

Stand Out on MLS

Only about 15% of Austin MLS listings include aerial photography. Leading with an aerial hero shot makes your listing visually distinct before buyers even read the description or look at interior photos.

🎬

Social-Ready Content

Drone footage is the most shared type of real estate content on Instagram and Facebook. A 60-second flyover clip gives agents ready-made social content that drives organic reach without paid promotion.

What to Look For

What to Look for When Hiring a Real Estate Drone Photographer in Austin

Not every drone operator is equal. Here's what separates professional real estate aerial photography from hobbyist footage:

🪪

FAA Part 107 Certificate

Commercial drone operations require an FAA Remote Pilot Certificate (Part 107). Always ask to see it. Flying commercially without a Part 107 cert is illegal — and any footage captured that way could expose you and your brokerage to liability.

🛡️

Liability Insurance

Professional drone operators carry aviation liability insurance, typically $1M+ per occurrence. Request a certificate of insurance before the shoot. Many HOA-gated communities and high-value listings require proof of insurance before allowing any aerial photography.

✏️

Post-Processing Included

Raw drone footage looks flat. MLS-ready photos require color grading, exposure correction, horizon leveling, and distortion removal. Confirm editing is included in the quoted price — not billed separately.

⚡

Fast Turnaround

Listing timelines are tight. A drone photographer who takes 3–5 days to deliver edited files can hold up your entire launch. Ceezaer delivers within 24 hours of every shoot.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does drone real estate photography cost in Austin?

Ceezaer packages start at $175 for properties under 3,000 sq ft (stills + 60-second clip). Larger properties, lake-front shoots, and twilight sessions are priced accordingly. Contact us for a custom quote.

Do I need FAA approval for my listing address?

Your photographer does — not you. Ceezaer pilots file all LAANC authorizations and notify local airspace authorities. You just need to ensure the airspace above the property isn't permanently restricted (most Austin residential areas are fine).

What happens if it's cloudy on shoot day?

Light overcast can actually be ideal for real estate aerials — it eliminates harsh shadows. If weather is unflyable (high wind, rain, storms), we reschedule at no charge, typically within 1–2 business days.

Can you also photograph the interior?

Ceezaer specializes in aerial and exterior photography. For interior photography, we can refer you to a trusted local real estate photographer who we coordinate with regularly for full-property packages.
