The Core Framework

8 Criteria for Evaluating a Construction Drone Analytics Platform

Evaluate every platform against these eight dimensions — in priority order. The first three are eliminators; if a platform fails them, stop evaluating.

1

AI Detection Quality (Eliminator)

The difference between drone platforms is primarily AI quality — not the drone hardware. Ask every vendor for a demo using your own site imagery, not their curated examples. Evaluate false positive rate (flagging non-issues) and false negative rate (missing real defects) separately. A platform with 80% recall (missing 20% of real anomalies) is not acceptable for safety or structural monitoring. Require minimum 88% recall on the defect categories relevant to your project type before shortlisting.

2

Report Format & Deliverable Quality (Eliminator)

Reports must be usable by superintendents in the field, not just by the platform vendor's customer success team. Evaluate whether reports export in PDF, are accessible on mobile, include GPS-linked photographs, and can be shared with owners and subcontractors without requiring platform access. If the report format requires interpretation training, it will not be used consistently. Test usability with your own PM team before committing.

3

Existing System Integration (Eliminator)

A drone analytics platform that doesn't connect to your existing project management software creates a data silo that gets abandoned within months. Require native integration with your current PM platform (Procore, Autodesk Build, Viewpoint, etc.) or a documented API with your vendor's integration support commitment. Ask specifically about bi-directional integration — can a flagged item in the drone platform automatically create an RFI in Procore? This is the difference between a monitoring tool and a decision engine.

4

Pricing Model & Cost Transparency

Drone analytics pricing varies enormously: some platforms charge per flight, others per project, others per user seat, others on annual enterprise contracts. Request a fully itemized pricing schedule including: base platform license, per-project fees, storage costs, overage fees for large projects, and any mandatory professional services for onboarding. Hidden fees are common — platforms that charge extra for AI features, storage beyond a low base tier, or "advanced" report formats are effectively more expensive than quoted.

5

Storage Architecture & Data Security

Construction site imagery contains proprietary project information. Evaluate: where data is stored (US-based data centers vs. international), encryption standards (AES-256 at rest minimum), access controls (role-based permissions), and data ownership terms. Some platforms claim perpetual license to use uploaded imagery for training purposes — a clause that is unacceptable for projects with NDA requirements or proprietary structures. Read the terms of service data provisions, not just the sales pitch.

6

Scalability for Multi-Project Portfolios

A platform that works for a single project may break down as you scale to managing 5–20 concurrent sites. Test multi-project portfolio views: can you see the status of all active projects on a single dashboard? Can you compare schedule adherence across projects? Does per-project pricing scale linearly (expensive) or is there a portfolio pricing tier? The platform that works for your first project should not require a renegotiation to work for your tenth.

7

Customer Support & Local Responsiveness

For construction operations, a 48-hour support ticket response time is unacceptable. When a drone imagery upload fails at 5pm on Thursday before a Friday owner meeting, you need same-day resolution. Evaluate: support hours, support channels (phone vs. email vs. chat), dedicated account manager availability, and whether there's a local representative or partner in your region who can provide hands-on assistance. For Austin-area GCs, working with Ceezaer means direct access to the team managing your specific flights — not a global support queue.

8

Training Data Breadth for Your Project Type

AI models trained primarily on data center or warehouse construction will perform poorly on residential subdivisions, bridge rehabilitation, or earthwork-heavy civil sites. Ask vendors specifically what project types their AI training datasets include, what geographies they cover (arid Texas construction sites look different from Pacific Northwest timber-frame work), and how often models are retrained with new data. A platform whose AI improves over time as you upload more projects is fundamentally more valuable than one with a static model.

Red Flags

Warning Signs When Evaluating a Platform

These are specific signals that a platform — or vendor — is not the right fit for a mid-market construction operation.

🚩

Demo Only Uses Vendor's Own Site Images

If a vendor refuses to run a demo on imagery you provide from your own project, they don't trust their AI to perform on real-world data outside their curated examples. This is the single strongest disqualifying signal in platform evaluation.

🚩

AI Features Behind a Paywall Tier

If the base platform is a photo viewer and the actual AI detection requires an "Enterprise" or "Pro" tier at a dramatically higher price point, the base product is not competitive. Compare true all-in pricing, not entry-level pricing that lacks the features you need.

🚩

No Construction-Specific Support Staff

A platform with customer success managers who've never been on an active construction site will struggle to help you apply the tool meaningfully. Ask during the sales process whether any of their support team has construction management experience.

🚩

Mandatory Annual Contract with No Pilot Option

Legitimate construction tech vendors offer a paid pilot program — typically 60–90 days on one project — before requiring annual commitment. Any vendor requiring a 12-month contract before you've validated the platform on your own projects is prioritizing their revenue over your success.

🚩

Data Rights Language That Transfers Ownership

Terms of service language permitting the platform to "use, analyze, and sublicense" your uploaded imagery for any purpose is a data ownership transfer disguised as a license. For projects with proprietary technology, legal agreements, or government security requirements, this is a contract-level problem, not a feature preference.

🚩

No Offline Mode for Field Report Access

Construction sites in Texas frequently have poor cellular connectivity. A platform that requires active internet access to view reports in the field isn't a field tool — it's an office tool. Offline access to recent reports and imagery is a non-negotiable feature for actual job site use.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Should we buy our own drone and a software platform, or use a managed service?
For most mid-market GCs, managed service wins on total cost and reliability. Hardware purchase + Part 107 training + software subscription + maintenance + ongoing pilot availability costs $15,000–$30,000 in year one with significant ongoing costs. A managed service at $1,500–$3,500/month per project delivers comparable or superior capability with zero capital investment and none of the operational burden of running a drone program internally. The breakeven for in-house programs typically requires 3+ simultaneous active projects with weekly coverage — a threshold most regional GCs don't meet consistently.
How long does platform onboarding typically take?
With Ceezaer, onboarding for a new project takes one week: initial call to collect project documents, baseline flight scheduling, and first report delivery. There is no software installation, no IT involvement, and no training curve for your team to receive and review reports. For clients integrating with Procore or Autodesk Build, the data connection setup takes an additional 2–3 business days.
Can we use our existing drone hardware with a new analytics platform?
Yes, most cloud-based drone analytics platforms accept imagery from any camera system — DJI, Autel, Skydio, or fixed-wing. However, "bring your own drone" programs require that you maintain FAA Part 107 compliance, scheduling, and quality control for the imagery. Inconsistent image overlap, altitude variation, or camera setting changes between flights significantly degrade the AI analysis quality.
What integration does Ceezaer offer with Procore?
Ceezaer delivers georeferenced orthomosaics and inspection reports in formats compatible with Procore's document management and drawing modules. AI-flagged anomalies can be formatted as Procore Observations or RFIs for direct import. We also provide Procore-shareable links for individual report items, allowing field teams to reference drone documentation directly within their Procore workflow without switching applications.
